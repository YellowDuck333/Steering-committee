Have your say on improving hospital discharge summaries

About the Project

This is a 3-year PhD project that aims to make discharge summaries clearer, so hospitals and care homes can work better together and provide better care for residents. The project is being hosted by the Vivaldi Network at University College London and is funded by the National Institute for Health and Care Research. It will be led by doctoral candidate Dr Calisha Allen.

The Research

The research has four parts:

  1. Understanding the health literacy skills of the social care workforce
  2. Understanding different views on discharge summaries from both hospital teams and social care professionals
  3. Writing guidance for writing hospital discharge summaries
  4. Exploring how AI (artificial intelligence) can help write discharge summaries

What is a steering committee?

A steering committee is a group that gives advice and shares ideas to help guide a project. The steering committee will include health and social care professionals, residents and representatives from social care and hospital discharge policy and research.

Social Care Professionals

We are looking for adult social care professionals who are passionate about making a change in social care. This is an opportunity to have your say on improving discharge summaries and through personalised coaching develop your skills in advocacy, public speaking and leadership.

You should:

  • Work in a non-nursing residential care home in England
  • Have completed your employer's mandatory training
  • Read hospital discharge summaries to provide care or share information from discharge summaries to those who provide direct care
  • Plan to stay in the care sector for at least the next 3 years
  • Be willing to learn to use Teams or Zoom for meetings and Microsoft Word, PowerPoint and Excel to read documents
  • Be passionate about social care and making care better for residents
  • Be committed to learning and self-development
  • Be interested in developing your skills to speak up and make a difference for social care including building your writing, speaking and leadership skills
  • Feel willing to speak in a group to share your thoughts
  • Not (and have not trained to be, or are training to) be a registered healthcare professional in the UK or abroad (e.g. nurse, physiotherapist, doctor etc.)

Desirable:

  • Interested in becoming a leader, researcher or advocate for others in social care

Requirements:

  • You will need to be able to work remotely and attend at least one in-person meetings in Manchester, Newcastle or London (travel will be covered).
  • You will need access to a tablet, laptop or computer
  • You must have a quiet and private place to work, as all sessions are confidential

Your role:

We are looking for care professionals with a range of experiences. You will need to be able to commit to join the project for the full 3 years. The role includes:

  • Attending about 8 steering committee meetings over three years (2-3 hours)
  • Joining 1 hour prep meetings before each steering committee
  • Support presenting at a 1-day Knowledge Share Event (face-to-face)
  • Giving some extra help to answer questions between meetings
  • Sharing your experiences of the project and coaching at the end of the project
  • Being fully engaged in the personal development opportunities provided

Benefits:

  • You will receive expert coaching, this will include 6 one-to-one coaching sessions and three action learning sets. This is designed to support your development as a member of the steering committee and future career ambitions within social care. Coaching will be individualised but can include public speaking skills, writing skills and advocacy skills.
